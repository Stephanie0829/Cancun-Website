MUSA, the underwater museum, was founded by Abraham, director of the national marine park. The sculptor Jason Taylor and others created over 500 underwater sculptures, aiming to show how art could be connected with the environment. The sculptures help increase biomass and create new habitats in the ecosystem. The sculptures are created using seabed and other beneficial materials to corals.

People can look through glass at the bottom of a boat in order to see the sculptures and art. Many people can also go scuba diving or snorkeling depending on the depth of the water.

There are three locations:

    Salon Manchones- sculptures are eight meters deep
    Salon Nizuc- sculptures are four meters deep
    Salon Punta Sam- sculptures are three and a half meters deep
